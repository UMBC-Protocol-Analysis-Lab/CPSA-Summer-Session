# Schedule

The workshop series will have synchronous meetings twice a week for 4 weeks, a total of 8 meetings. Meeting are on **FILL IN THE DATES AND TIME HERE**. The first several lectures include foundational cryptography concepts. Each week is generally separated into an introductory lecture on theory, and then a lecture on CPSA and applications.

## Week 1

LO1: By the end of week 1, students should be able to understand and reason about basic cryptographic concepts, including: strands, keys, encryption, decryption, hashing, messages, protocols, and who the adversary is.

### Session 1

- What is a protocol  
- Cryptography intro  
- Dolev-Yao adversary  
- Adversary actions (crypto primitives)  
- Designing a meeting protocol on whiteboard  
- [Homework 1](http://homeworks/homework1.md): Design a protocol of your own (for another scenario)

### Session 2

- Strand spaces  
- Needham-Schroeder protocol  
- Protocol analysis  
- CPSA tutorial for protocol analysis  
- [Homework 2](http://homeworks/homework2.md): Improve your protocol by using CPSA

## Week 2

LO2: By the end of Week 2, students will be able to use CPSA to model protocols based on simple specifications. Students will be able to use CPSA to find attacks in example protocols, and reason about how those attacks happened, and how to fix them.

### Session 1

- Spotting attacks in CPSA  
- Fixing attacks in CPSA  
- Model example program in CPSA  
- Maybe something other than PAKE-0? (PAKE unless good alternative)  
- [Homework 3](http://homeworks/homework3.md): Model several “known-bad” protocols that we design, and use CPSA to identify the flaw(s)

### Session 2

- Needham-Schroeder Key Exchange  
- Assumptions in CPSA  
- Channels in CPSA  
- [Homework 4](http://homeworks/homework4.md):  
  - FIDO

## Week 3

LO3: By the end of Week 3, students will be able to read and understand protocol speciifcations for real-world protocols. Students will then take those protcols and turn them into CPSA models to find more subtle flaws.
